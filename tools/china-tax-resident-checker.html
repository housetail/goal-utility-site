Determine your China tax-resident status using the 183-day presence test and the 6-year rule for worldwide income.

Frequently asked questions

What is the 183-day rule?

If you are physically present in China 183 days or more in a tax year, you are a tax resident for that year.

What is the 6-year rule?

After 6 consecutive resident years (without a single 30-day or longer absence in a year), your worldwide income is taxed in China.

Does the clock reset?

A single year with a continuous absence of 30 or more days resets the 6-year count.
