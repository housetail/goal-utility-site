Frequently asked questions

Who pays IIT in China as a foreigner?

Both tax residents (the 183-day rule) and non-residents pay IIT on China-sourced income. Residents are also taxed on worldwide income; non-residents only on China-sourced income.

What is the 5,000 RMB monthly threshold?

It is the basic deduction (基本减除费用). Residents can further deduct social insurance & housing fund (五险一金) and special additional deductions (专项附加扣除) before computing tax.

Do social insurance & housing fund (五险一金) apply to foreigners?

In most cities foreigners join pension, medical and unemployment insurance plus the housing fund. Rates and base caps differ by city — e.g. Beijing 2026 caps the social base at 7,162–35,811 RMB.

Can expats claim special additional deductions (专项附加扣除)?

They are available to tax residents. Non-residents generally cannot claim them, so keep the toggle on "Resident" if you qualify.

How is the tax computed?

Residents use the cumulative withholding method (累计预扣法) on a progressive annual table, which correctly handles monthly variable bonuses. Non-residents use the simplified monthly method.

Is this an official calculation?

No. It is an estimate for planning only. Verify rates, bases and rules with the local tax bureau (税务局) and a qualified advisor.
